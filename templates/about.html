About SatTrack

This Web Application takes the input from the user and database for a desired Indian satellite and based on the inputs specified by the user, projects the orbital positions on the Earth’s surface to generate the sensor footprint.

Following are the inputs which can be provided by the user to the application to guide them for determining the imaging opportunities:

1) Coordinates of point of interest in terms of Latitude and Longitude on Earth.
2) Sensor within the payload.
3) Possible swath which can be imaged with the particular sensor.
4) Tilt of the satellite within the known agility of the platform.
5) Orbital parameters like semi-major axis, eccentricity, inclination, longitude of ascending node, argument of perigee and true anomaly based on the publicly available information in the form of Two Line Elements (TLE).

Objective :

The objective is to find the possibility or predict the time of imaging a particular object/place (Lat-Long) on Earth by an Earth observing satellite with given sensor specifications, orbit and attitude definitions under various orbit perturbations.

Background :

Low Earth observation satellites orbit the Earth surface at an altitude of around 300 to 1000 km in sun-synchronous polar orbit. The sensors are placed on the satellite platform to take pictures of the Earth’s surface. Users are interested in the opportunities when their area of interest is going to be imaged to meet their application requirements. This software-based application enables them to know the imaging opportunities a priori for their planning. Agile satellite platforms have the capability to image certain areas with tilts which are defined by revisit constraints. This application guides the user for imaging their area of interest taking into account the tilt requirements. This information can be shared with the satellite pass programming personnel.
